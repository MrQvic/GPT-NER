Czech Workers in the Reich
The Czechs were never keen to
do slave labour in the Reich.
While the number of workers
who went to the Reich from the
German inhabited frontier areas
of Czechoslovakia was between
300,000 and 400,000—the un-
employment problem was solved
there by this means—the number
of Czechs was substantially
lower. In September, 1941,
there were approximately 140,000
Czech and 80,000 Slovak workers
working in German factories and
there has been no considerable
change in these figures since.
