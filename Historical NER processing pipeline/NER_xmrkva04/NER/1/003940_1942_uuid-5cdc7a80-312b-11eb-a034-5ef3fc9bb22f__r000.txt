coming in at a much greater rate
than transports could be sent to

Tourists' House in Cracow no
longer sufficed to accommodate

negotiations, the military camp
in Bronowice was allocated to
Czechoslovak military personnel.
At that time General Sergej Ingr
left the country and went abroad
to take over the command of the
growing Czechoslovak Army.
From that moment onwards it
is difficult in a brief account to
follow the adventures and desti-
nies of the Czechoslovak soldiers.

France. While in Bronowice the
