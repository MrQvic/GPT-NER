1. National enterprises are under public ownership,
as defined in subsequent regulations, and they have
the status of independent bodies corporate. They are
regarded as firms enjoying all rights, and subject to
taxation as firms publishing annual accounts. They
must pay duty according to the legal rules, and duty
equivalents from the date of their establishment, as
per art. 1, par. 2, a), of the Act of April 8, 1938,
No. 76 on duty equivalents.
2. The net value of the substance transferred to
a national enterprise by the State, constitutes its
original capital. The day when the national enter-
prise is vested with the property belonging to it, will
be promulgated in the official gazettes.
